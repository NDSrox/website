The Glass Door Project

In my house, there are some cabinets with glass panels in the doors, through which you can see the contents. Unfortunately, all but one of the pieces holding one of these panes were broken, leaving the square of glass in a precarious, wobbly, situation. One day, I decided to remove the last piece, take it to school, and measure it. Using Tinkercad, I made a nearly identical replica, relying on my (super cool) digital caliper for precise measurements. I asked my friend to slice it for me on Cura (I can't use any good slicer software on my Chromebook, so he lets me use his Macbook). I 3D printed them at home, and it came out really well. I used the screws that held the broken bits of the pieces that formerly held the glass in place to put in my new pieces. Now, my parts are reliably keeping the panel in place!
